# Sold ’Em — Overview

- Sold ’Em is a multiplayer card trading game where players buy and sell cards through auction to build the best poker hand. Players compete across multiple rounds, with rankings based on cumulative PnL.
- The goal is to maximize your total PnL across all rounds by winning pots and auctioning cards strategically.
- There are five suits: the conventional clubs, diamonds, hearts, and spades, as well as the Citadel suit.  
  There are 10 cards per suit, numbered 1–10, for a total of 50 cards.
- Players are grouped into tables of 5\. Each player starts with 200 chips and 5 random cards. Each person places an ante of 40 chips into the pot at the start of each round.
- Each round consists of orbits (typically 3). Within each orbit, players take turns auctioning cards. You can auction more than one card; the winner chooses one to keep. The game ends after all orbits complete, and players reveal their hands at showdown.
- Bids occur privately. That is, everyone places bids independently and only the winner’s bid is revealed.
- In case of ties, the person who bids first wins. Bids must happen within 10 seconds.
- The very first auction is a house auction. The money spent on the auctioned card goes into the main pot. Winning the house auction also gives you the right to auction off your cards first.
- For the rest of the auctions, the person who auctions their card is paid the winning bid by the person who wins the auction. The winner of an auction is the one who bids the highest, and they pay their bid—in other words, this is a first-price auction.
- There are various game modes and variants that are expounded upon in the rules tab of the game:
  - Distribution of the pot after showdown may vary based on game mode such as winner-takes-all, high-low hands, and more.
  - Depending on the round, there may be community cards which are shared among all players.
  - There are also round-specific rules on bidding on your own cards when it is your turn to auction.
- Stacks reset each round, but total PnL accumulates across all rounds. Final rankings are determined by total PnL and rounds won.
